Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SwiftDocs really free?

Yes! SwiftDocs is completely free with no hidden costs, in-app purchases, or subscriptions. All features are available to all users.

Where are my files stored?

All your files are processed and stored locally on your device. SwiftDocs does not upload files to any server or cloud storage. Your documents remain completely private.

Do I need an internet connection?

No! SwiftDocs works completely offline since all processing happens on your device. You don't need an internet connection to use any features.

What file formats are supported?

SwiftDocs currently supports:

  • PDF files
  • Images (JPG, PNG, HEIC, WebP)
  • Documents (with conversion capabilities)

We're constantly adding support for more formats based on user feedback.

How do I report a bug?

Please email us at lakshyarohila21@gmail.com with:

  • A description of the issue
  • Steps to reproduce the problem
  • Your device model and OS version
  • SwiftDocs app version (found in Settings)

Can I request a feature?

Absolutely! We love hearing feature ideas from our users. Send your suggestions to lakshyarohila21@gmail.com.

Is my data safe?

Yes! Since all processing happens locally on your device and we don't have any servers, your data never leaves your device. We can't access your files even if we wanted to.

Why doesn't SwiftDocs require an account?

We believe in simplicity and privacy. Since we don't store any data on servers, there's no need for accounts. Just download and start using the app immediately.

What permissions does the app need?

SwiftDocs only requests permissions necessary for its functionality:

  • Storage: To read and save files on your device
  • Camera: To scan documents (optional)

Troubleshooting

The app is crashing

Try these steps:

  1. Force close the app and reopen it
  2. Restart your device
  3. Make sure you have the latest version of SwiftDocs
  4. Check that you have sufficient storage space
  5. If the issue persists, contact support

File conversion failed

If a file conversion fails:

  • Ensure the file isn't corrupted
  • Check that you have enough free storage space
  • Try with a smaller file first
  • Verify the file format is supported

App is slow or unresponsive

Performance tips:

  • Close other apps running in the background
  • Process smaller files or batches
  • Ensure your device has sufficient free storage
  • Keep your device OS up to date
